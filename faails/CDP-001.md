## The Problem — The Articulation Gap

The biggest gap in AI today is not capability.

It is the gap between human intent and human articulation.

A person with a revolutionary vision but limited technical vocabulary gets a limited product — not because AI cannot build it, but because AI only executes what was said, not what was meant.

A 15-year-old with a world-changing idea does not have the vocabulary to fully specify it. An 80-year-old with 60 years of accumulated wisdom cannot always translate it into instructions an AI will execute faithfully. A founder with an enormous vision gives imprecise language and gets an imprecise product.

The AI did exactly what it was told. That is the problem.

No existing AI system closes this gap. They all execute what was said. ONXZA executes what was meant.

---

## The Solution — The Board Session

Before any significant vision is executed, a board session runs.

Multiple specialized agents reason together — not sequentially, but simultaneously — to bridge the gap between what was said and what was meant.

Agents challenge each other's interpretations. Flag inconsistencies. Surface implications the human did not see. Ask strategic clarifying questions — maximum two per message, never a wall of questions. Reason until they actually understand the intent behind the words.

This is not a single agent asking clarifying questions. This is a multi-agent board that thinks together about what the human actually needs, then surfaces the gap back to the human in a form they can verify and approve.

The output is a refined, agent-validated vision that reflects what the human **means** — not just what they said.

## The Protocol — How a Board Session Works

### Trigger
Any vision intake that meets one or more of these conditions:
- The stated vision is ambiguous or internally inconsistent
- The vision has implications the human may not have considered
- The technical requirements are underspecified
- The business model or success criteria are unclear
- The scope could be interpreted in materially different ways

### Participants
A board session assembles the agents most relevant to the vision type:

| Vision Type | Board Participants |
|---|---|
| Product / software | Architect, PM, Legal, Security |
| Business / company | CEO, Finance, Legal, Marketing PM |
| Content / media | Content PM, Brand, Marketing PM |
| Technical system | Architect, Backend, DevRel |

Plus always: the company AgentDeveloper and MG_Parent_Orchestrator.

### Session Structure

**Round 1 — Independent Analysis**
Each board agent independently reads the vision statement and writes:
- What they understand the intent to be
- What is ambiguous or underspecified
- What implications the human may not have seen
- What constraints or risks are not addressed

**Round 2 — Cross-Agent Review**
Agents review each other's analyses. Flag disagreements. Identify consensus. Surface the most important gaps — not every gap, only those that would cause downstream failure.

**Round 3 — Consolidated Question Set**
Board produces a single consolidated set of clarifying questions for the human. Maximum five questions total. Ordered by importance. Each question is specific, not abstract.

**Round 4 — Human Response**
Human answers. Board processes the answers. If answers resolve all gaps: proceed to vision document creation. If new gaps emerge: one additional round only. No infinite loops.

**Round 5 — Refined Vision**
Board produces a refined vision statement that reflects the human's original intent plus the clarifications gathered. Presented to human for final approval. Human approves or corrects. Once approved: vision.md is created and locked.

---

## The Output — Refined Vision Document

The output of every board session is a vision.md file that:
- Quotes the human's original statement verbatim
- Documents the board's interpretation
- Records all clarifications gathered
- States what was assumed and why
- Defines success at 30 / 90 / 180 days
- Identifies non-negotiables
- Lists known constraints
- Surfaces the forward vision (what comes after the target)
- Names required departments and agents

This document becomes immutable once the human approves it. No agent below Marcus can modify it. It is the north star the entire execution chain follows.

## Board Failure Modes

Not every board session reaches consensus smoothly. This section defines how the protocol handles failure scenarios.

### Scenario 1: Board Disagreement on Round 3 Questions

**What happens:** Board agents disagree on which questions are most important or cannot agree on the consolidated question set.

**Resolution:**
- Marcus (or the Orchestrator agent) has final casting vote authority
- Marcus reviews all Round 2 analyses and the disagreement
- Marcus produces the final Round 3 question set
- Questions proceed to the human (Round 4)
- This is not escalation — Marcus serves as tie-breaker, not higher authority

**Principle:** Board is advisory, not consensus-required. Marcus has authority to move forward.

### Scenario 2: Human's Round 4 Answers Create New Ambiguity

**What happens:** Human's answers in Round 4 resolve some gaps but introduce new, equally important questions.

**Test:** Does the answer set close more gaps than it opens?
- If YES (more closed than opened): proceed to Round 5 normally
- If NO (equal or more opened): escalate to Rule 3 below

**Resolution:**
- Board may optionally request one synchronous conversation with the human
- This is not an async round — it is a synchronous meeting or call
- Goal: resolve the new ambiguity in real-time
- If synchronous conversation is not possible or does not resolve: escalate to Rule 4

### Scenario 3: Board Member Unavailable or Non-Responsive

**What happens:** One or more board members is unavailable or does not respond to provide analysis in a round.

**Resolution:**
- Board may proceed with available members
- Missing member's perspective is noted in the records
- If the missing member's domain is critical (e.g., Security for a security-sensitive vision), Marcus may delay the round
- If delay is not possible: proceed without that member; Marcus notes the risk

### Scenario 4: Irresolvable Ambiguity (Maximum Escalation)

**What happens:** After Round 4 (or optional synchronous conversation), core ambiguities remain unresolved. The vision cannot be refined to an actionable state.

**Definition of irresolvable:** After good-faith analysis in Round 2 and clarification in Round 4, one or more of these remain true:
- The core objective is still ambiguous (what are we actually building?)
- Success criteria are undefined and cannot be made concrete
- Constraints are contradictory and human cannot choose between them
- The vision has internal logical inconsistencies the human refuses to resolve

**Resolution:**
1. Board produces a formal summary of unresolved gaps
2. Vision is placed in `CDP-BLOCKED` status
3. Ticket is created with `requires_aaron: true`
4. Summary is presented to Aaron with recommendation: pause until human clarifies, OR build with documented ambiguity and resolve later
5. Aaron decides: clarify first, or proceed with documented risk
6. No vision.md is created until clarification is resolved

**Timeline protection:** If a CDP board session exceeds 4 hours of wall time without reaching Round 5, Marcus has authority to place the vision in `CDP-BLOCKED` and request Aaron's direct involvement (conversation, not async rounds).

### Scenario 5: Board Member Disagrees Strongly with Refined Vision

**What happens:** In Round 5, one board member believes the refined vision does not reflect the human's intent.

**Resolution:**
- Board member must provide specific objection and alternative interpretation
- If human confirms their intent matches the refined vision: objecting member may abstain or accept majority decision
- If human agrees with objecting member's interpretation: Round 5 revises to incorporate the feedback
- Debate does not halt progress — Marcus decides if revision is needed or if objection is noted and proceeded with documented disagreement

### Timeline Guardrails

| Phase | Max Duration | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Round 1 (Independent Analysis) | 1 hour | Board |
| Round 2 (Cross-Agent Review) | 1 hour | Board |
| Round 3 (Consolidated Questions) | 30 min | Marcus |
| Round 4 (Human Response + Board Processing) | 1.5 hours | Board + Human |
| Optional: Synchronous Conversation | 30 min | Marcus + Human |
| Round 5 (Refined Vision) | 30 min | Board |
| **Total** | **4 hours max** | Marcus |

If total time exceeds 4 hours, Marcus escalates to Aaron for direct intervention.

### When to Escalate to Aaron

Create a ticket with `requires_aaron: true` in these cases:
1. Vision is in `CDP-BLOCKED` status (Scenario 4)
2. Board and human cannot resolve new ambiguity from Round 4 (Scenario 2 with synchronous conversation failing)
3. Total CDP session time exceeds 4 hours with no path to Round 5

In all escalation cases, Aaron will either:
- Request additional clarification from the human (synchronous call)
- Accept the vision with documented ambiguities
- Reject the vision and ask for a restart with different framing
