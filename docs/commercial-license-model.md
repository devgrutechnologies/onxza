## 1. Philosophy

ONXZA is open source. That is a distribution strategy, not a charity model.

The free tier builds the community. The community builds the ecosystem. The ecosystem sells the Pro and Enterprise tiers. Every developer who installs ONXZA for free is a potential paying customer when they hit production scale — or when their employer asks them to deploy it for real revenue-generating work.

**The rule:** Core functionality is free for everyone. We charge for commercial deployment, scale support, white-label rights, and SLA guarantees. We never gate features that developers need to evaluate and love the product.

## 4. What "Commercial Use" Means

Commercial use is any deployment of ONXZA where the operating entity:
- Generates revenue directly or indirectly from agent operations
- Deploys agents on behalf of clients or customers for compensation
- Uses ONXZA in a production environment that serves a commercial business

**Community tier is explicitly allowed for:**
- Personal projects and learning
- Open-source projects (even if the project accepts donations)
- Academic research and publication
- Non-profit organizations
- Internal evaluation and proof-of-concept (up to 90 days)
- Startups pre-revenue (up to $0 ARR — honor system, no enforcement)

**Community tier is NOT allowed for:**
- Running agents that generate affiliate revenue
- Using ONXZA to deliver client services for pay
- Deploying in a production system for a commercial business
- OEM or white-label in a paid product

## 7. Skills Marketplace Revenue Split

Skills published to the ONXZA marketplace:
- **Community skills (free):** MIT license. No revenue share needed. Community attribution.
- **Premium skills (paid):** 70% to skill author, 30% to DevGru Technology Products.
- **Certified skills (audited by DTP):** Highlighted in marketplace. DTP earns 30% on all sales.

This creates a revenue-sharing ecosystem. Developers can earn by building skills. DevGru earns by being the marketplace. Community wins when good skills exist.

## 8. Enforcement Philosophy

ONXZA is self-hosted. We cannot technically enforce commercial license compliance at the binary level. We do not want to — that would damage community trust.

**Our enforcement model:**
- Honor system for pre-revenue startups
- License check on `onxza init` (accepts --license-key flag for Pro/Enterprise)
- Annual renewal reminder emails
- Clear Terms of Service that commercial use without license is a violation
- No DRM, no phone-home, no telemetry without explicit opt-in

We earn compliance through trust, great support, and value delivery — not through technical enforcement. Companies that love ONXZA will license it. Companies that don't love it shouldn't be forced to pay.
